What is Sleep Apnea?

Understanding Sleep Apnea Symptoms & Treatment

Sleep apnea is a serious sleep disorder where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. This comprehensive guide covers symptoms, causes, and effective treatment options available. Sleep apnea affects millions of Americans and can significantly impact quality of life, energy levels, and overall health if left untreated.

There are three main types of sleep apnea: obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), which is the most common form caused by throat muscles relaxing; central sleep apnea, where the brain doesn't send proper signals to breathing muscles; and complex sleep apnea syndrome, which is a combination of both types.

Common symptoms of sleep apnea include loud snoring, episodes of stopped breathing during sleep witnessed by others, gasping for air during sleep, morning headaches, difficulty staying asleep, excessive daytime sleepiness, difficulty concentrating, and irritability. Many people are unaware they have sleep apnea until a partner notices their breathing patterns.

Learn how CPAP and BiPAP therapy can improve your sleep quality and overall health. Our respiratory therapy experts provide personalized solutions for better sleep. Treatment typically involves lifestyle changes, oral appliances, CPAP therapy, or in severe cases, surgery. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial for preventing serious health complications.
